<p><ul><li>Adhesive-bonded CLT</li><li>Mechanically Fastened CLT</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cross-laminated timber (CLT) can be classified into two main types based on how the layers are bonded together: adhesive-bonded CLT and mechanically fastened CLT. Adhesive-bonded CLT involves applying adhesive to the layers of timber to bond them together, creating a strong and stable panel. On the other hand, mechanically fastened CLT uses mechanical fasteners such as screws or nails to join the timber layers. Both types of CLT have their own advantages and are used in various construction applications based on project requirements.</p></p>
