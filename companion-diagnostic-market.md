<p><p>The companion diagnostic market can be categorized into two main types: immunohistochemistry (IHC) and molecular diagnosis. IHC focuses on identifying specific proteins or biomarkers in tissue samples, aiding in targeted therapy decisions. Molecular diagnosis involves genetic testing to detect mutations or alterations in DNA or RNA, guiding personalized treatment options. Together, these methods enhance precision medicine by ensuring that patients receive therapies tailored to their unique biological profiles, thereby improving treatment outcomes and reducing adverse effects.</p></p>
