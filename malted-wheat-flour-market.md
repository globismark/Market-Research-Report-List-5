<p><p>The malted wheat flour market is categorized into diastatic and non-diastatic types. Diastatic malted wheat flour contains active enzymes that promote fermentation and enhance flavor, making it ideal for baking and brewing. In contrast, non-diastatic malted wheat flour lacks these enzymes, serving primarily as a flavoring agent and for color enhancement in baked goods. Both types are utilized in various food products, contributing unique taste profiles and textures, thereby catering to different consumer preferences and applications in the food industry.</p></p>
