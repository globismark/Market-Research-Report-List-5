<p><p>Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) is a trenchless rehabilitation method used to repair existing pipelines without the need for extensive excavation. This process involves inserting a resin-saturated felt tube into the damaged pipe, which is then cured using hot water, steam, or UV light, resulting in a new pipe lining that restores structural integrity and functionality. The CIPP method is widely used in wastewater, stormwater, and drinking water pipelines due to its minimal disruption to surrounding infrastructure.</p><p>The CIPP market is anticipated to experience significant growth, driven by increasing urbanization, aging pipelines, and the need for sustainable infrastructure solutions. <p><strong>The Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Inversion Type</li><li>Pull-in Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) is a trenchless rehabilitation method for repairing existing underground pipelines. The Inversion Type involves inverting a resin-saturated liner through the existing pipe, allowing it to cure in place, forming a new inner pipe. The Pull-in Type, on the other hand, involves pulling the liner into the host pipe and then curing it. Both methods enhance pipe integrity and extend the service life of aging infrastructure, offering efficient and minimally invasive solutions for pipeline repair and maintenance.</p></p>
