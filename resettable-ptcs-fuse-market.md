<p><p>Resettable PTC fuses, or Polymeric Positive Temperature Coefficient fuses, are classified primarily into two types: polymer and ceramic. The polymer type utilizes a polymer matrix with conductive fillers that provide overcurrent protection by increasing resistance when exposed to excessive heat, allowing them to reset once cooled. In contrast, ceramic types typically feature a ceramic substrate that offers improved thermal stability and higher current ratings, making them suitable for demanding applications. Both types play critical roles in safeguarding electronic devices and circuits.</p></p>
