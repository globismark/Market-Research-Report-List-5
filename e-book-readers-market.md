<p><p>E-book readers are primarily categorized into two types based on their screen technology: E Ink and LCD screens. E Ink screens use electrophoretic display technology, resembling printed paper, which reduces eye strain and enhances battery life, making them ideal for reading long texts. In contrast, LCD screens offer vibrant color displays, making them suitable for multimedia content and interactive features. The choice between the two often depends on user preferences, such as reading habits and the type of content consumed.</p></p>
