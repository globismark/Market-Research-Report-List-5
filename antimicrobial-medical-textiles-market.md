<p><p>The antimicrobial medical textiles market comprises primarily two types: non-woven and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are created by bonding fibers together through methods like chemical, mechanical, or thermal processes, offering versatility and cost-effectiveness for disposable medical products such as surgical masks and gowns. Woven fabrics, on the other hand, are made by interlacing yarns, providing durability and strength, ideal for long-lasting applications like hospital linens and uniforms. Both types incorporate antimicrobial properties to reduce infections and enhance patient safety.</p></p>
