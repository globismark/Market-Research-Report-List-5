<p><p>The ammonia gas sensors market comprises various types, each with distinct mechanisms. Metal semiconductor sensors utilize changes in electrical conductivity when exposed to ammonia. Conductive polymer sensors leverage the conductivity variations in polymers upon gas interaction. Electrochemical sensors detect ammonia through chemical reactions generating measurable electric currents. Nanomaterial-based sensors harness the unique properties of nanostructures to enhance sensitivity. Optical fiber sensors exploit light transmission changes caused by ammonia presence. Other types may include membrane-based or specialized sensors tailored for specific applications.</p></p>
