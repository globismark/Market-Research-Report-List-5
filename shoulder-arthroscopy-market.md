<p><p>Shoulder arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat various shoulder conditions, including rotator cuff tears, shoulder instability, and impingement syndrome. It involves inserting a small camera called an arthroscope into the shoulder joint through tiny incisions, allowing surgeons to visualize the internal structures and perform repairs without the need for extensive open surgery. This technique offers numerous benefits, including reduced pain, shorter recovery times, and lower risk of complications.</p><p>The Shoulder Arthroscopy Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by an increasing prevalence of shoulder disorders, a growing elderly population, and rising participation in sports and physical activities.
