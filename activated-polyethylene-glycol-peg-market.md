<p><p>The Activated Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) market is categorized into several types based on molecular structure. Linear PEGs are the most common, featuring straight-chain configurations that facilitate drug delivery and bioconjugation. Branched PEGs offer enhanced solubility and flexibility due to their tree-like structure. Multi-arm PEGs contain multiple reactive ends, allowing for cross-linking and complex drug formulations. Other variations may include functionalized or specialty PEGs used in specific applications like diagnostics or therapeutic formulations, expanding the versatility of PEG in biomedicine.</p></p>
