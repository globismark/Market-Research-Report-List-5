<p><p>The gastrointestinal electrocardiograph market comprises two primary types: Electrogastroenterography (EGEG) and Cutaneous Electrogastrography. EGEG involves the use of specialized electrodes placed on the abdomen to measure gastric electrical activity, providing insights into gastrointestinal motility and disorders. Cutaneous Electrogastrography, on the other hand, is a non-invasive technique that utilizes surface electrodes to record electrical signals from the stomach, making it suitable for broader applications and patient comfort. Both methods help in diagnosing and monitoring gastrointestinal conditions effectively.</p></p>
