<p><p>The passenger vehicle steel wheel market consists of three main types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten steel into molds to form wheels, offering flexibility in design and cost-effectiveness for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping steel under high pressure, resulting in stronger, more durable wheels suitable for high-performance vehicles. Other methods may include processes like welding or specialized treatments, catering to specific requirements or niche markets. Each type serves unique automotive needs.</p></p>
