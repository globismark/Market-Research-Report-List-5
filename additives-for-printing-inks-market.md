<p><p>The additives for printing inks market is categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic additives are primarily derived from carbon-based compounds, enhancing properties like flow, adhesion, and color stability. They include surfactants and plasticizers, vital for optimal ink performance. In contrast, inorganic additives are mineral-based and improve durability, opacity, and viscosity. Common examples include pigments and fillers, which help in achieving consistent quality and longevity in printed materials, catering to diverse printing applications.</p></p>
