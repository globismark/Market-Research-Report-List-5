<p><p>The skin imaging systems market is divided into two primary categories: ultrasound-based and optical-based systems. Ultrasound-based skin imaging utilizes sound waves to create detailed images of skin layers, offering insights into deeper tissues and aiding in the diagnosis of various skin conditions. In contrast, optical-based systems rely on light to capture surface features and pigmentation, providing valuable information for cosmetic applications and skin cancer detection. Both types serve critical roles in dermatology, cosmetic analysis, and research.</p></p>
